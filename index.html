Gur e Amir Mausoleum

The Gur-e Amir or Guri Amir is a mausoleum of the Asian conqueror Tamerlane also known as Timur in Samarkand, Uzbekistan. It occupies an important place in the history of Turko-Persian Architecture as the precursor and model for later great Mughal architecture tombs, including Humayun's Tomb in Delhi and the Taj Mahal in Agra, built by Timur's Persianised descendants. It has been heavily restored.
